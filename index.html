Teeth & Smiles in Art

From Renaissance portraits to contemporary pop culture, the depiction of teeth and smiles reveals much about beauty, class, fear, and the human condition across the centuries.

A century-by-century journey from the 15th-century "empty mouth" (teeth symbolising carnality) through the Japanese Edo practice of blackening teeth, Europe's association of smiling with low status, to the 20th-century rise of the open, fluoride-bright smile we know today.

Oral Health Guide

How to Protect Your Teeth

Your teeth are meant to last a lifetime. Here are the evidence-based habits that keep them strong, white, and healthy.

🪥
Brush twice daily
Brush for at least 2 minutes, morning and night, using a fluoride toothpaste. Use a soft-bristled brush at a 45° angle to the gumline and use gentle circular motions.
🧵
Floss every day
Flossing removes plaque and food between teeth where your brush can't reach. Slide gently between each tooth and curve around the tooth base. An interdental brush works too.
🚰
Use fluoride products
Fluoride strengthens enamel and protects against decay. Use fluoride toothpaste and consider a fluoride mouthwash if your dentist recommends it.
🍎
Watch what you eat
Limit sugary and acidic foods. Bacteria feed on sugar to produce acids that erode enamel. Crunchy fruits and vegetables like apples can naturally help clean teeth.
💧
Drink plenty of water
Water — especially fluoridated tap water — washes away food particles and bacteria. It also prevents dry mouth, which can accelerate tooth decay.
🦷
Wear a mouthguard
If you play contact sports or grind your teeth at night (bruxism), a custom mouthguard from your dentist protects against chips, cracks, and wear.
🚭
Avoid tobacco
Smoking and chewing tobacco stain teeth, cause gum disease, and dramatically increase your risk of oral cancer. Quitting is the single best thing you can do for oral (and general) health.
📅
Regular dental check-ups
Visit your dentist every 6–12 months for a professional clean and examination. Early detection of cavities, gum disease, and other issues saves time, money, and discomfort.
🌙
Protect enamel at night
Wait 30 minutes after eating before brushing — especially after acidic foods — to let saliva neutralise acids. Never go to bed with sugary drinks or snacks.
